SABAH

Mount Kinabalu

Mount Kinabalu, the tallest peak in Southeast Asia, rises majestically in Sabah, Malaysia, offering breathtaking views and diverse ecosystems. Standing at 4,095 meters, this UNESCO World Heritage site is home to unique flora and fauna, including rare orchids and the iconic Rafflesia. A favorite among hikers and nature lovers.

Selingan Island

Selingan Island, also known as Turtle Island, is a serene sanctuary off the coast of Sabah, renowned for its conservation efforts to protect endangered sea turtles. Visitors can witness turtles nesting and participate in releasing hatchlings, all while enjoying the island's pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Sepilok Orangutan Rehab Centre

Sepilok, located in Sabah, is a world-renowned sanctuary dedicated to the rehabilitation of orphaned and injured orangutans. Set within a lush rainforest, the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre offers visitors a chance to observe these gentle primates up close in their natural habitat.

Sipadan Island

Sipadan Island, located off the coast of Sabah, is one of the world’s premier diving destinations, famous for its crystal-clear waters and extraordinary marine biodiversity. This protected island offers divers a chance to explore vibrant coral reefs, swim alongside sea turtles, and encounter schools of barracudas and reef sharks.

Bohey Dulang Island

Bohey Dulang Island, located in the Tun Sakaran Marine Park, is a stunning destination in Sabah known for its picturesque scenery and vibrant marine life. The island's crystal-clear waters and coral reefs make it a perfect spot for snorkeling and diving, while its lush landscape offers scenic hiking trails with breathtaking views.

Danum Valley

Danum Valley, located in Sabah, is a pristine rainforest reserve known for its rich biodiversity and ancient trees. This remote sanctuary offers visitors a chance to explore lush jungles, spot rare wildlife like orangutans and pygmy elephants, and experience the untouched beauty of one of Borneo's most important conservation areas.

Kinabalu Park

Kinabalu Park, located in Sabah, is a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its stunning biodiversity and the towering Mount Kinabalu. This park offers a variety of eco-tourism experiences, from challenging hikes to scenic walks, showcasing diverse plant and animal species within its rich, mountainous landscape.

Maliau Basin

Maliau Basin, often referred to as Borneo’s Lost World, is a remote and pristine conservation area in Sabah, Malaysia. Known for its enigmatic basin shape and rich biodiversity, it offers visitors a rare glimpse into untouched rainforests and unique wildlife.
