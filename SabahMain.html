About

Sabah

Sabah, often celebrated as 'The Land Below the Wind,’ is nestled on the northern tip of Borneo, where majestic mountains and serene seas meet. This vibrant region is a tapestry of stunning landscapes, from the soaring heights of Mount Kinabalu to the idyllic shores of its tropical islands. Sabah’s diverse environments are home to rich wildlife, from elusive pygmy elephants to colorful marine life, while its cultural heritage is a mosaic of indigenous tribes and modern influences. Here, ancient rainforests, bustling markets, and tranquil village life intertwine, offering a unique glimpse into the dynamic beauty and heritage of this enchanting corner of the world.

Topography


Sabah’s topography is a stunning blend of dramatic landscapes, featuring the towering Mount Kinabalu and the rugged Crocker Range in the central highlands, which are surrounded by lush rainforests and rich biodiversity. The coastal plains and mangrove swamps to the west and north contrast with the fertile lowlands, while Sabah’s tropical islands and vibrant coral reefs offer breathtaking marine vistas. The state’s extensive river systems and picturesque waterfalls add to its natural allure, creating a diverse and dynamic environment that supports a wide array of ecosystems and wildlife.

UNESCO World Heritage Site


Sabah is home to the Kinabalu Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, recognized for its outstanding biodiversity and the iconic Mount Kinabalu. This protected area spans a range of ecological zones, from tropical lowlands to alpine meadows, and supports a diverse array of plant and animal species. It is noted for its remarkable geological formations, unique flora, and rich biodiversity, making it a significant conservation area and a key destination for ecological and geological research.

PEOPLE and CULTURES


Sabah is a vibrant mosaic of ethnic diversity and cultural richness, home to a variety of indigenous groups such as the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, and Rungus, each with their unique traditions and festivals. The state’s cultural tapestry is reflected in its lively festivals, traditional crafts, and diverse cuisine, which includes local delicacies like hinava and tuhau. Sabah’s social harmony is evident in its blend of languages, including Malay and various indigenous dialects, and its religious diversity encompassing Islam, Christianity, and traditional beliefs. This cultural fusion creates a dynamic and harmonious community, offering a rich and immersive experience for visitors.
