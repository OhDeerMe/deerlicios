Activies in

SARAWAK

Bako National Park

Bako National Park, the oldest national park in Kuching Sarawak, and renowned for its diverse wildlife and dramatic coastal landscapes. The park's stunning rock formations, secluded beaches, and dense mangroves make it a popular destination for nature lovers and hikers.

Carpenter Street, Kuching

Carpenter Street in Kuching is a historic and bustling street known for its rich cultural heritage and charming old-world architecture. Lined with traditional shophouses, the street offers a mix of local eateries, vibrant markets, and artisanal shops

Waterfront, Kuching

Waterfront Kuching is a picturesque riverside area in the heart of Kuching, Sarawak, offering a scenic promenade along the Sarawak River. The waterfront comes alive at night with a stunning display of lights, creating a vibrant and enchanting atmosphere.

Cultural Village, Kuching

Kampung Budaya in Kuching, Sarawak, is a vibrant cultural village that celebrates the diverse heritage of Malaysia through traditional arts, crafts, and performances. It offers visitors an immersive experience into the rich cultural tapestry of Sarawak’s indigenous communities.

Lambir Hills, Miri

Lambir Hills National Park, located near Miri, Sarawak, is known for its rich biodiversity and stunning rainforest landscapes. The park offers a variety of hiking trails, leading to beautiful waterfalls and scenic viewpoints, perfect for nature enthusiasts and birdwatchers looking to explore Sarawak's natural beauty.

Siniawan, Kuching

Siniawan Night Market, located in Kuching, Sarawak, is a charming weekend market known for its vibrant atmosphere and delicious local food. The market is set along a historic old-town street, offering a variety of traditional dishes and snacks, as well as live music. It's a popular spot for both locals and tourists to enjoy Sarawak's culture and street food scene.

Sibu Central, Market

Sibu Central Market, located in Sibu, Sarawak, is the largest indoor market in Malaysia, The market is known for its vibrant atmosphere, featuring everything from fruits, vegetables, and seafood to traditional Iban handicrafts. It's a bustling hub that showcases the diverse culture and culinary richness of the region.

MULU National Park

Mulu National Park in Miri is renowned for its stunning limestone karst formations, extensive cave systems, and rich biodiversity. The rainforest is teeming with diverse wildlife and plant species, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering a remarkable adventure for those seeking natural beauty and exploration.

Niah National Park

Niah National Park, renowned for being the site of the oldest human remains found in Southeast Asia, is a must-visit destination for history and nature enthusiasts. The park has series of impressive caves, each holding significant archaeological treasures and offers an unforgettable journey into both history and nature.
