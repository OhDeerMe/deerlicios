Must try foods in

SARAWAK & SABAH

Tomato Mee, Kuching Sarawak

Tomato Mee is a popular dish in Kuching, Sarawak, known for its unique and flavourful take on noodles. It is a noodle dish featuring a rich and savory tomato-based sauce. Tomato Mee stands out because of its tangy and slightly sweet tomato sauce, which provides a different taste experience compared to other noodle dishes.

Laksa, Sarawak

Laksa Sarawak is a popular and beloved dish from the Malaysian state of Sarawak, located on the island of Borneo. It’s a distinctive type of laksa that stands out from other versions found throughout Southeast Asia due to its unique blend of flavors and ingredients. Laksa Sarawak is a flavorful noodle soup that combines elements of Chinese and Malay cuisine, resulting in a dish that is rich, aromatic, and deeply satisfying.

Kolo Mee, Sarawak

Kolo Mee, a signature dish of Sarawak, is renowned for its simplicity and rich flavor. Often enjoyed as a breakfast staple, this beloved noodle dish features tender minced meat and slices of succulent barbecued pork, garnished with fresh spring onions and crispy fried shallots. Its popularity has led to the creation of a Halal version, which substitutes the traditional pork with tender slices of beef or chicken.

Tuaran noodle, Sabah

Tuaran noodles, a specialty from the town of Tuaran in Sabah, are a unique and beloved dish known for their springy texture and rich flavor. These yellow egg noodles are typically handmade, fried to a crispy golden hue, and then stir-fried with a mix of vegetables, meats like pork or chicken, and sometimes seafood. What sets Tuaran noodles apart is the use of fresh egg yolks in their preparation, which gives them a distinctive taste and texture.

Sang Nyiuk Mian, Sabah

Sliced Pork Noodles are a popular dish in Sabah, known for their rich, comforting flavors and tender meat. This dish typically features thin slices of succulent pork served over springy egg noodles, accompanied by a flavorful broth made from pork bones and aromatic herbs. The noodles are often topped with green onions, crispy fried garlic, and a dash of soy sauce, giving it a savory depth. Sometimes, the broth is enhanced with a light, peppery flavor, balancing the richness of the pork. Sliced Pork Noodles are a favorite comfort food in Sabah, offering a satisfying and hearty meal enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Sinalau Bakas

Sinalau Bakas is a popular traditional dish in Sabah, particularly iconic among the Kadazandusun people, the largest ethnic group in North Borneo. The name comes from the Kadazandusun words "Sinalau," meaning "smoked," and "Bakas," meaning "wild boar." This delicacy is commonly sold at roadside stalls and in 'Tamu' markets across the west coast and interior regions, including Kota Belud, Ranau, Tambunan, Keningau, and Telupid. One of the most well-known areas to find Sinalau Bakas stalls near Kota Kinabalu is along the highway from Tamparuli to Kundasang, on the route to Mount Kinabalu.

Fish Soup Noodles

Sabah Fish Noodles are a beloved dish in Sabah, known for their light, refreshing flavors and fresh seafood ingredients. The dish typically features tender white fish slices, fish paste, or fish balls served with rice noodles in a clear, delicate broth made from fish stock. The broth is often flavored with ginger, tomatoes, and preserved vegetables, creating a subtle yet savory taste. Some versions come with a choice of clear, milky, or spicy soup bases. The noodles are often garnished with green onions and fried shallots, adding extra layers of texture and flavor.

Mixed Beef Noodle

Mixed Beef Noodles in Sabah is a hearty and flavorful dish, celebrated for its combination of tender beef cuts and savory broth. The dish typically includes various parts of beef, such as slices of lean meat, beef balls, tendon, and tripe, served over egg or rice noodles. The rich, aromatic broth is usually simmered with beef bones, spices, and herbs, creating a deep, comforting flavor. Often garnished with fresh green onions, fried garlic, and a dash of soy sauce, the dish is served with a side of chili sauce for added heat.
